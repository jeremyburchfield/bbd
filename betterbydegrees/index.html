Talent Changes Everything

About Talent Dividend Initiative

The mission of the ChattanoogaRegion Talent Dividend (CRTD) is to engage business and higher education to collaboratively develop a sustainable quality workforce. The CRTD supports business competitiveness, profitability, and regional economic development. It shapes the culture of quality degree completion importance resulting in the Chattanooga region as the place of choice for business operations.

Who We Are

Talent Dividend-The Chattanooga Way is a collaboration of educational institutions and business firms for the purpose of increasing per capita the number of citizens in the region who attain a post-secondary degree. This collaboration provides a regional focus as it directs attention away from isolated institutions and business firms toward a regional grouping.



Goals & Initiatives

To facilitate the regions communities to achieve at least a 1% increase, tri-annually, in the number of citizens who have a post-secondary degree creating a sustainable, high quality work force.

To recruit business and higher education institutions to become engaged in the CRTD including “expositional learning” and, if appropriate, certification of training programs for college credit and for outsourcing management of tuition reimbursement programs.

To engage the business sector and accredited higher education institutions (public and private) in collaborative activities of embedding critical business skill sets in courses leading to improve quality degrees to meet business needs.

To design and implement an ongoing marketing campaign streamlining completion of college among non-traditional students (24-64 years of age) with some college but no degree.

To monitor and report progress of higher education institutions regarding degree completion supply and embracing the culture of degree completion importance through tri-annual celebrations throughout the tri-county region.

To assure relevant policies and governance through the existing registered Advisory Panel and a 501 (c) 3 organization.

What We Do

The Chattanooga Region Talent Dividend Initiative facilitates the collaboration of businesses, individuals seeking higher education, and government to achieve the aims of strategic decisions and helps establish a distinctive advantage for our community.

Convening Business and Industry Groups
Convening of business and industry groups, educational institutions regarding quality of graduates, as well as skills/competencies required by business and industry.

Facilitating and Referring
Facilitating and referring services including portfolio development (Credit for Prior Learning); advisory assistance; selecting a degree program and enrollment.

Connecting Students to Resources
Connecting students interested in pursuing a post-secondary degree with the resources they need.

Interfacing with Community Organizations
We interface with chambers of commerce regarding economic development and workforce development to maximize economic growth

Council for Adult Experiential Learning (CAEL)

The Council for Adult Experiential Learning (CAEL), a national organization, has been a leader for 39 years in connecting learning and work for adults. CAEL’s focus is to help solve the mounting national problem of increasing the number of post-secondary credentials among adults.

CAEL research shows that adults who complete a Prior Learning Assessment (PLA) and earn college credit are 2 1/2 times more likely to persist to graduation. 43% of adults who complete the PLA earned a bachelor’s degree, compared to 15% of adults who did not complete PLA.

Without PLA, 6% completed the associate’s degree while 13% of those who completed PLA completed the associate’s degree.

In other words, 56% of adults who complete PLA completed at least a post-secondary degree while 21% of adults not completing PLA achieved at least a post secondary degree.
